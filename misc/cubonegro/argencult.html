How to tell if you're Argentinian

Before reading this stuff, notice that I (Pablo Barenbaum) am an exception to many of this cultural patterns, starting by the fact that I dislike football... this tries to describe the culture of a society, not of an individual.

Also, consider that I'm trying to represent the middle urban/suburban class.

If you're Argentinian

  • You're familiar with

  • You know how football (soccer) is played. If you're male, you probably see the matches on Sundays and argue about them on Mondays. You can't tell why Americans think soccer is boring but still like baseball.

  • You should get from one to four weeks of vacation a year, if you work with a legal background. If not, you're lucky if you can get vacations at all.

  • It's fairly likely that you're a Christian. If you don't believe in God, people feel uncomfortable about it.

  • McDonald's, Burger King, etc. are moderately cheap food.

  • You probably own a telephone and a TV. Your place is heated in the winter and has its own bathroom. You do your laundry in a machine. You don't kill your own food. You eat at a table, sitting on chairs.

  • You don't consider insects, dogs, cats, monkeys, or guinea pigs to be food. You probably eat meat twice a week or more. You don't eat as much fish as you know you should.

  • A bathroom may not have a bathtub in it, but it certainly has a toilet.

  • Telephone, railroads, airlines, gas and oil services used to be public, and you thought they didn't work fine. Now they are privatized, and you think they don't work fine.

  • Home telephones work fine. Two of three street telephones don't.

  • Trains that work are quite a good service. However, railroads don't really connect each city with another, but each one with Buenos Aires (the Capital). Moreover, many of them have been closed.

  • There used to be two great parties, but this is changing. Nowadays there are countless; you know and vote for politicians, not for parties (not in a literal sense). You expect them at least not to steal public money.

  • You'd respect someone who speaks English, French or German. You understand Portuguese if people speak it slowly, and you believe you understand Italian, so you think they aren't worthy to learn.

  • You must know English if you want to get a job, use a computer or travel to any foreign country.

  • School and college have public and private variants. If you want a decent education, you should pay for a private school; public college is still better than private. Elementary school is mandatory.

  • College, depending on the career, is from three to five years long.

  • Mustard comes in plastic jars. Shaving cream comes in cans. Milk comes in plastic bags or in cardboard boxes.

  • The date comes first: 25/5/1810 (and you know what happened on that date).

  • The decimal point is a comma. A dot is for marking thousands.

  • World War II was a problem for your country; staying neutral had bad consequences.

  • The Falklands War was a tragedy, where drunk generals sent young men to die. You've probably seen someone who fought in the War selling Argentinian flags to do for a living. Though you might believe your country has the rights on the islands, you think the problem should be solved by other means.

  • You expect marriages to be made for love, not arranged by third parties. Most marriages of religious people happen in church. A man gets one woman at a time.

  • If a man has sex with another man, either he's a homosexual, or he's in prision and is raping/being raped by other prisioners as part of the "criminal code".

  • Once you're introduced to someone, unless the other person is in a higher position than you (i.e. your boss), you can call them by their first name.

  • If you're a woman you don't go to the beach topless.

  • A hotel room might have a private bath or a common one.

  • Unless you're a kid, you'd rather a film subtitled than dubbed. Most foreign movies are American.

  • You expect to be able to do business or deal with the government without paying bribes. Under a minor fault, tipping a police officer is common.

  • If a politician has been cheating on his wife, you don't really mind.

  • Just about any store will take your credit card.

  • A company can fire just about anybody it wants, paying him an indemnization.
