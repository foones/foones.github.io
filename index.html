Research

I have recently been appointed as an Investigador Asistente, a permanent research position, at CONICET, with affiliation to Universidad Nacional de Quilmes (UNQ).

I am a member of the LoReL team, the Logics and Dynamics of Programming Languages Group of the LIA SINFIN, and the FunLeP group. I hold teaching positions at Departamento de Computación and UNQ.

Previously, I defended my PhD in Computer Science at Departamento de Computación (Facultad de Ciencias Exactas y Naturales, Universidad de Buenos Aires). My advisors were Eduardo Bonelli and Delia Kesner. The main topic of my thesis was evaluation strategies in calculi of explicit substitutions at a distance. Most of my PhD was funded by a CONICET grant.

My research interests gravitate towards the lambda calculus and its various manifestations, including rewriting theory, type theory, proof theory, and the foundations and implementation of programming languages and proof-assistants.

Software

I have been involved in the development of PyGobstones (2011–2012) and Gobstones Web (2017–2018). These are free software tools that implement the Gobstones programming language.

The Gobstones programming language was designed by a group of researchers and teachers at the University of Quilmes, lead by Pablo "Fidel" Martínez López, to teach students without any previous exposure to programming.
